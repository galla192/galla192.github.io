ATLAS and Team HKU-DRC

I began my profesional life with technology as an engineer. My first job was as an intern with Team HKU-DRC competing in the DARPA Robotics Challenge. With very little experience I packed up from my home in dreary East Lansing and flew around the world to tropical Hong Kong to work with the cutting edge humanoid robot ATLAS. It didn't take very long to become a trusted member of the team. I learned from truly brilliant engineers how to approach difficult problems in programming and how to break down seemingly impossible tasks into their component parts. I assisted the team lead in giving ATLAS a simple sense of touch and successfully piloted him through an early qualifying task. I learned a huge amount about what it took to be a professional in a tech field and because of that I wouldn't trade my experience in Hong Kong for the world

My First School

My first 2 years of college I attended the University of North Carolina at Pembroke and played soccer for the varsity team. I loved North Carolina and their relative lack of winter plus they wanted me to play soccer all day with gifted players so naturally I was in heaven. The learning curve was steep as a 17 year old playing with grown men but I quickly learned that a mental focus and maturity was required that I did not have at 17. But over my time there I developed the mindset required for success and I took that home with me to Michigan. I made many lasting friendships and learned as much as I could while I was there but I realized that it wasn't for me. The school offered none of the major programs I was interested in plus the lure of friends and family back home was irresistable. It became clear that despite the amazing experiences I'd had there that it was time to go home and become a Spartan
